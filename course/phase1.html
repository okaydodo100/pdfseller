Lesson 1

Why 90% of People Fail Before They Start

Most people pick niches based on what THEY find interesting. That's the fastest way to fail.

I made this mistake. My first product was about "Morning Routines for Productivity." I was obsessed with morning routines. I thought everyone else would be too.

Result: 2 sales in 3 months. Total disaster.

My second product was "Remote Work Tax Mastery" ‚Äî a topic I found boring. But I noticed thousands of people desperately asking questions about it online.

Result: 142 sales in the first month. $3,834 from a "boring" topic.

The difference? I stopped choosing topics I liked and started choosing topics people would PAY for.

Lesson 3

The Reddit Research Method (Step-by-Step)

Reddit is a goldmine for finding problems people will pay to solve. Here's exactly how to use it:

Step 1: Use Google Site Search

Don't use Reddit's search ‚Äî it's terrible. Use Google instead:

site:reddit.com [your niche] "how do I"

Other search phrases that find paying customers:

  • ‚Üí site:reddit.com [niche] "I'm struggling with"
  • ‚Üí site:reddit.com [niche] "help me"
  • ‚Üí site:reddit.com [niche] "I can't figure out"
  • ‚Üí site:reddit.com [niche] "what's the best way to"
  • ‚Üí site:reddit.com [niche] "I've tried everything"

Step 2: Look for High-Engagement Posts

Find posts with 50+ comments. High engagement = validated demand.

If a post has hundreds of comments with people sharing the same frustration, you've found a profitable problem.

Step 3: Mine the Comments for Gold

The comments are more valuable than the posts. Look for:

  • ‚Üí Solutions people tried ‚Äî What's NOT working? Your product can fix that.
  • ‚Üí Specific questions ‚Äî These become chapters in your course.
  • ‚Üí Exact phrases ‚Äî Copy these for your sales page. Speak their language.
  • ‚Üí Emotional language ‚Äî "frustrated," "desperate," "at my wit's end" = buyers

üéØ Real Example:

When researching "remote work taxes," I found these Reddit posts:

  • ‚Üí "How do I handle taxes working from home?" (347 comments)
  • ‚Üí "Freelancer tax nightmare - help!" (189 comments)
  • ‚Üí "What can I deduct as a remote worker?" (156 comments)
  • ‚Üí "I got audited for my home office - what now?" (203 comments)

This told me: HUGE demand, lots of confusion, people desperate for clear answers. I built a product around exactly these questions.

‚úÖ Your Action Step:

Right now, do 5 Reddit searches in your potential niche. Find at least 3 posts with 50+ comments. Copy the exact phrases people use to describe their problem. You'll use these later.

Lesson 4

Facebook Group Research

Facebook users are generally older and more likely to have money than Reddit users. Here's how to research:

Step 1: Find Relevant Groups

Search Facebook for groups related to your niche. Join 5-10 groups with 10,000+ members (50,000+ is ideal).

Step 2: Search Within Groups

Use the group's search bar to find pain points:

  • ‚Üí Search: "help"
  • ‚Üí Search: "struggling"
  • ‚Üí Search: "frustrated"
  • ‚Üí Search: "how do I"
  • ‚Üí Search: "advice"

Step 3: Identify Post Types

Look for these high-value posts:

  • Question Posts (20+ comments): People asking for help = they'll pay for solutions
  • Frustration Posts: "I've tried everything and nothing works" = desperate buyers
  • Success Posts: "Finally figured it out!" = validates the problem is solvable

Bonus: You can see poster profiles to understand demographics ‚Äî age, job, location. This helps you speak directly to your buyer.

Lesson 5

Demand Validation (The 10-Minute Test)

Before committing to a niche, run these 3 quick validation checks:

Check 1: Google Trends

Go to trends.google.com and search your topic.

  • ‚úÖ Good: Stable or rising trend, score 50+ average
  • ‚ùå Bad: Declining trend, score under 25

Check 2: Amazon Book Search

Search Amazon for books on your topic.

  • ‚úÖ Good: Multiple books with BSR under 100,000
  • ‚úÖ Good: Recent books (published in last 2 years) ranking well
  • ‚ùå Bad: No books, or all books have BSR over 500,000

Check 3: Existing Paid Products

Search Google for: [your niche] course or [your niche] guide

  • ‚úÖ Good: 3-5 existing products at $17-97 price point
  • ‚úÖ Good: Products with testimonials and reviews
  • ‚ùå Bad: Zero paid products (no proven demand)

üí° Key Insight:

Competition is GOOD. It proves people pay for solutions. Your job is to be better, more specific, or more up-to-date ‚Äî not to find a niche with zero competition.

Lesson 6

Competition Analysis (Find Your Angle)

Find 3-5 competitors and analyze them. Your goal: find gaps you can fill.

What to Analyze:

  • Their angle: Who specifically are they targeting?
  • Their reviews: What do people complain about? (1-star and 3-star reviews are gold)
  • What's missing: What questions aren't they answering?
  • Their format: Is it outdated? Too long? Too short?

7 Ways to Differentiate:

1. Specificity

"Taxes" ‚Üí "Taxes for Freelance Designers"

2. Recency

"Updated for 2025 tax laws"

3. Format

Video where others have only text

4. Speed

"Get results in 7 days"

5. Simplicity

"No jargon, plain English"

6. Completeness

"Everything in one place"

7. Guarantee

"Works or money back"

Lesson 7

The Niche Selection Matrix

Score your potential niche on these 5 factors (1-10 each):

Factor Score 1-10 What 10 Looks Like
Demand ____ Hundreds of posts, 50+ comments each
Pain Level ____ Desperate language, urgent need
Buyer Intent ____ People already buying similar products
Competition ____ Competitors exist but have clear weaknesses
Createability ____ You can research and organize a solution

Scoring Guide:

  • 40-50 points: Green light. Start immediately.
  • 30-39 points: Good with the right angle. Proceed with differentiation.
  • 20-29 points: Needs more research. Might work with pivot.
  • Below 20: Find a different niche.
