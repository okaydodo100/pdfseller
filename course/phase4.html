Lesson 2

The Sales Page Structure

Your sales page will have these sections, in this order:

1. Hero Section

Headline, subheadline, call-to-action button

2. Problem Section

Describe their pain in vivid detail

3. Solution Section

Introduce your product as the answer

4. What's Inside Section

List all modules, lessons, bonuses

5. Benefits Section

What they'll achieve (outcome-focused)

6. Social Proof Section

Testimonials, results, credibility

7. Pricing Section

The offer, price, guarantee

8. FAQ Section

Handle objections and concerns

9. Final CTA Section

Last push, urgency, final button

Lesson 3

Writing Headlines That Convert

Your headline is the most important text on the page. If it doesn't grab them, they'll leave.

Headline Formulas That Work:

  • The "How To" Formula:
    "How To [Achieve Desired Result] Without [Thing They Fear]"
  • The "Finally" Formula:
    "Finally: A [Solution] That Actually [Works/Delivers Result]"
  • The "What If" Formula:
    "What If You Could [Desired Outcome] In Just [Timeframe]?"
  • The "The System" Formula:
    "The [Adjective] System For [Achieving Result] (Even If [Objection])"

Examples:

  • ‚Üí "How To Master Your Taxes as a Remote Worker (And Keep Thousands More)"
  • ‚Üí "Finally: A Productivity System That Works FOR Your ADHD Brain"
  • ‚Üí "What If You Could Have Difficult Conversations Without the Drama?"
  • ‚Üí "The Simple System For Losing Weight Without Counting Calories"

Prompt for Headlines:

Create 10 headline options for a sales page about [YOUR TOPIC].

Target audience: [WHO THEY ARE]
Their main problem: [PROBLEM]
The result they want: [DESIRED OUTCOME]

Use these formulas:
- How To [result] Without [fear]
- Finally: A [solution] That Actually [works]
- What If You Could [outcome] In Just [timeframe]?
- The [adjective] System For [result]

Make them specific, benefit-focused, and attention-grabbing.
Lesson 4

Writing the Problem Section

This section makes visitors feel understood. Use the exact phrases from your research.

Prompt for Problem Section:

Write a compelling problem section for my sales page about [TOPIC].

Target audience: [WHO]
Their daily frustrations: [LIST FROM RESEARCH]

Real quotes from my audience research:
"[Quote 1]"
"[Quote 2]"
"[Quote 3]"

Write copy that:
1. Opens with a question that hooks them ("Ever feel like...?")
2. Describes their daily struggle in vivid detail
3. Acknowledges what they've tried that didn't work
4. Names the emotional toll (frustration, shame, overwhelm)
5. Ends with hope: "But it doesn't have to be this way..."

Make them feel like you're reading their mind. Use "you" language.
Approximately 300-400 words.

Lesson 6

Social Proof (Even If You're New)

If you don't have testimonials yet, here's what to use instead:

Social Proof Alternatives:

  • Stats about the problem: "73% of remote workers overpay on taxes..." (use real stats from your research)
  • Your own results: "I used this system to..." (if you have personal experience)
  • Research citations: "Studies show that..." (adds credibility)
  • Beta tester feedback: Give free access to 3-5 people in exchange for honest feedback
  • Trust badges: Secure checkout, money-back guarantee, privacy protected

‚ö†Ô∏è Never Fake Testimonials

It's unethical and will destroy trust if discovered. Use real results or skip testimonials and focus on other proof elements.

Lesson 7

The Pricing Section

This is where you present your price and make it feel like a no-brainer.

Pricing Section Elements:

  • ‚Üí Value stack: List everything they get with "values" (adds up to way more than price)
  • ‚Üí The price: Clear, prominent, no hiding
  • ‚Üí Price anchoring: Compare to alternatives (coaching, courses, trial/error cost)
  • ‚Üí Guarantee: 30-day money-back guarantee removes risk
  • ‚Üí CTA button: Clear action ("Get Instant Access")

Prompt for Pricing Section:

Write a pricing section for my sales page.

Product: [NAME]
Price: $[PRICE]

What's included:
- [Item 1]
- [Item 2]
- [etc.]

Create a value stack showing what each component "would be worth" separately, then reveal the actual price as a deal.

Include:
- Comparison to alternatives (coaching costs $X, courses cost $Y)
- 30-day money-back guarantee copy
- Urgency/scarcity if applicable (but keep it honest)
- Strong CTA button text

Make the price feel like a no-brainer investment.
