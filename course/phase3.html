Lesson 2

GitHub Setup (Step-by-Step)

GitHub stores your website files. Think of it as a folder in the cloud that Vercel can access.

Step 1: Create GitHub Account

  1. 1. Go to github.com
  2. 2. Click "Sign up"
  3. 3. Enter your email
  4. 4. Create a password
  5. 5. Choose a username (this will be visible, pick something professional)
  6. 6. Complete the verification puzzle
  7. 7. Verify your email

Step 2: Create a New Repository

  1. 1. Once logged in, click the green "New" button (or the + icon in top right)
  2. 2. Name your repository (example: my-course)
  3. 3. Set it to "Public" (required for free Vercel hosting)
  4. 4. Check "Add a README file"
  5. 5. Click "Create repository"

Step 3: Upload Your Course Files

  1. 1. In your new repository, click "Add file" ‚Üí "Upload files"
  2. 2. Drag your entire course folder (all HTML files) into the upload area
  3. 3. Wait for upload to complete
  4. 4. Add a commit message like "Add course files"
  5. 5. Click "Commit changes"

üí° Important:

Your main page MUST be named index.html. This is the file that loads when someone visits your site. Make sure it's in the root of your repository (not inside a subfolder).

‚úÖ Your Action Step:

Create your GitHub account and upload your course files right now. Don't continue until your files are in GitHub.

Lesson 3

Vercel Setup (Step-by-Step)

Vercel takes your GitHub files and turns them into a live website. It's automatic and free.

Step 1: Create Vercel Account

  1. 1. Go to vercel.com
  2. 2. Click "Sign Up"
  3. 3. Choose "Continue with GitHub" (important ‚Äî this connects them)
  4. 4. Authorize Vercel to access your GitHub

Step 2: Import Your Repository

  1. 1. Click "Add New..." ‚Üí "Project"
  2. 2. You'll see your GitHub repositories listed
  3. 3. Find your course repository and click "Import"
  4. 4. Leave all settings as default
  5. 5. Click "Deploy"

Step 3: Wait for Deployment

  1. 1. Vercel will build and deploy your site (takes 30-60 seconds)
  2. 2. Once done, you'll see "Congratulations!" with a link
  3. 3. Click the link to see your live website!
  4. 4. Your URL will be something like: your-project.vercel.app

üéâ Your site is now LIVE!

Anyone can access it at the vercel.app URL. But we want a professional custom domain, which we'll set up next.

üí° Automatic Updates:

Whenever you update files in GitHub, Vercel automatically redeploys your site. You never need to manually update ‚Äî just push changes to GitHub.

Lesson 4

Buying Your Domain (Namecheap)

A custom domain (like yourbrand.com) makes you look professional and legitimate. Namecheap has the best prices.

Step 1: Find Your Domain

  1. 1. Go to namecheap.com
  2. 2. Search for your desired domain name
  3. 3. Aim for a .com if possible (most trusted)
  4. 4. Expect to pay $8-15/year for a .com domain

Step 2: Purchase the Domain

  1. 1. Add desired domain to cart
  2. 2. Create a Namecheap account (or log in)
  3. 3. At checkout, TURN OFF all extras (they're not needed)
  4. 4. Turn ON "WhoisGuard" (free privacy protection)
  5. 5. Complete purchase

Naming Tips:

  • ‚Üí Keep it short and memorable
  • ‚Üí Easy to spell (no weird variations)
  • ‚Üí Relates to your niche or brand
  • ‚Üí Avoid hyphens and numbers if possible

Lesson 5

Connecting Domain to Vercel

Now we connect your custom domain to your Vercel site. This is the trickiest part ‚Äî follow exactly.

Step 1: Add Domain in Vercel

  1. 1. Go to your Vercel dashboard
  2. 2. Click on your project
  3. 3. Go to "Settings" tab
  4. 4. Click "Domains" in the sidebar
  5. 5. Enter your domain name (e.g., yourdomain.com)
  6. 6. Click "Add"
  7. 7. Vercel will show you DNS records to configure

Step 2: Configure DNS in Namecheap

  1. 1. Log in to Namecheap
  2. 2. Go to "Domain List"
  3. 3. Click "Manage" next to your domain
  4. 4. Find "Nameservers" section
  5. 5. Select "Custom DNS"
  6. 6. Enter Vercel's nameservers:
    ns1.vercel-dns.com ns2.vercel-dns.com
  7. 7. Click the checkmark to save

Step 3: Wait for Propagation

  • ‚Üí DNS changes can take up to 48 hours (usually much faster)
  • ‚Üí Typically works within 15-30 minutes
  • ‚Üí Vercel will show "Valid Configuration" when ready
  • ‚Üí Your site will now work at yourdomain.com!

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Issue:

If it's not working after 30 minutes, try visiting your domain in an incognito/private browser window. Sometimes your browser caches the old DNS.

Lesson 6

Stripe Setup (Accept Payments)

Stripe lets you accept credit card payments from customers worldwide. No monthly fees ‚Äî just 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction.

Step 1: Create Stripe Account

  1. 1. Go to stripe.com
  2. 2. Click "Start now"
  3. 3. Enter your email and create password
  4. 4. Complete verification (they'll ask for basic info)

Step 2: Activate Your Account

  1. 1. Click "Activate payments" in your dashboard
  2. 2. Fill in your business details (you can use your name if no business)
  3. 3. Add your bank account for payouts
  4. 4. Verify your identity (they may ask for ID)
  5. 5. This process takes 1-2 days for approval

Step 3: Create a Payment Link

  1. 1. In Stripe dashboard, go to "Payment Links" (in left sidebar)
  2. 2. Click "Create payment link"
  3. 3. Enter your product name (your course name)
  4. 4. Set the price ($27, or whatever you're charging)
  5. 5. Click "Create link"
  6. 6. Copy your payment link ‚Äî this is what customers click to pay!

Step 4: Configure Success Redirect

  1. 1. In your payment link settings, find "After payment"
  2. 2. Select "Redirect to your website"
  3. 3. Enter your course URL (e.g., yourdomain.com/course)
  4. 4. Now when someone pays, they automatically get sent to your course!

üí° The Flow:

Customer clicks "Buy Now" on your sales page ‚Üí Goes to Stripe checkout ‚Üí Pays ‚Üí Automatically redirected to your course. That's it!
